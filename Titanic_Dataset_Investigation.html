Titanic Dataset Investigation¶

This report will try to explore the Titanic dataset.This dataset contains 12 variables. According to the introduction of the dataset on Kaggle, the variables can be described as below:

PassengerId: ID of passenger
survival:Survival   0 = No, 1 = Yes
pclass: Ticket class    1 = 1st, 2 = 2nd, 3 = 3rd
Name: Name of passenger
sex: Sex
Age: Age in years
sibsp: # of siblings / spouses aboard the Titanic
parch: # of parents / children aboard the Titanic
ticket: Ticket number
fare: Passenger fare
cabin: Cabin number
embarked: Port of Embarkation   C = Cherbourg, Q = Queenstown, S = Southampton Variable Notes

Variable Notes

pclass: A proxy for socio-economic status (SES)d
1st = Upperd
2nd = Middled
3rd = Lowerd

age: Age is fractional if less than 1. If the age is estimated, is it in the form of xx.5d

sibsp: The dataset defines family relations in this way
Sibling = brother, sister, stepbrother, stepsisterd
Spouse = husband, wife (mistresses and fiancés were ignored)d

parch: The dataset defines family relations in this way
Parent = mother, fatherd
Child = daughter, son, stepdaughter, stepsond
Some children travelled only with a nanny, therefore parch=0 for them.d

Questions to Explore¶

For this Titanic dataset, there are many interesting things to explore. First, there are three kind of ticket classes in Titanic. People with different ticket classes should stay in different location of Titanic and they may also have different difficulty levels of reaching life-saving equipment. Therefore, this report will first try to explore that do people in different ticket classes have different survivor rate？

Besides, many stories about Titanic show that many male passengers help female passengers and even give their own survive chances to the female passengers. This report will also try to explore that is this true?

In addition, people will different age may have different difficulty levels to survive during disasters. Does this also show in Titanic? This report will also explore how age affects survivor rate.

Also, people from the same family should be more likely to help each other than strangers, so dose the number of parents or children aboard the Titanic affects the survivor rate?

Finally, this report will also try to explore that do these variables have some correlations with each other?

summary¶

As we can see from the analysis above, in order to analysis how the survival rate of Titanic is influenced by various factors, I first perform data wrangling. The two missing embarked values are filld based on the fare values. The instances with missing age values are deletd and the cabin column is deleted. There are too many missing values in cabin column, which makes it unhelpful for analysis. In the analysis section, we can see that the ticket class have positive influence on the survivor rate. People with better ticket class have a higher survivor rate. It's reasonable, since people in better ticket class may have better location and better reachment for the life-saving equipments. An interesting thing is, when we add sex factor in this analysis, we can see that male passengers in first class have much higher survivor rate than male passengers in the second and third classes, and survivor rate of male passengers in second and third class are similar. Female passengers have much higher survivor rate compared with the male passengers in all the three classes, especially female passengers in the first and second classes. The story that many male passengers in Titanic help female passengers and even sacrifice their own life may be true. We can also see that most female survivor's ages are around 15-40. Most male survivors' age are around 20-30. People with higher or lower age may be more difficult to survive, because of their own health or strength situation. Also, we can see that people with more number of parents or children are more likely to survive. Maybe because family will be more likely to help each other than strangers. Besides, an obvious econimic theory is also shown by the fare and the number of people embarked in some certain locations. The more people choose, the higher the fare of the embark location. Finally, we also find that some variables have some relationship with each other. For example, the lower number/better ticket class, the higher the age. Maybe because the elder people are richer than young people. The lower number/better ticket class, the higher the fare. People with the more the number of siblings / spouses, the more the number of parents/children......

However, there may be still many factors behind the data and many unexploied situations. This report still has many limitations. For furthur study, deeper machine learning methods should be involved in the research. The survival rate should be better quantified and more analysis methods should be involved in the divided training and testing dataset.
